Bio

I am an artist, writer and performer with a socially engaged practice. My work frequently deals with place, the self and identity. As an entryway to these themes I tend to use my personal context as an entryway. As such, the work is informed by Irish/Northern Irish, post-conflict and rural contexts. In terms of form, I typically use video, sound and spoken word. I also work using interactive systems and radio.

In January 2024 I graduated from University of Limerick's MA in Art & Technology and am now based nearby Newry City, Northern Ireland. I am currently participating in PS2 Belfast's Ideal Development Opportunity residency and working as a volunteer on Locate International's development programme.

Artworks and projects

Homehouse: Exploring everyday connection to place (DAWN, Shannon Rowing Club, Limerick City, April 2023)
This audiovisual installation focuses on my mother's native area in South Armagh. It includes interviews, field recordings and video material, which reflect my experience of re-engaging with the area and various people within it. The final artwork questions the role of the artist in the process.

different things (Adhesion, Spacecraft Studios, Limerick City, April 2023)
This video artwork combines field recording and home video footage to interrogate the items on display around the family home.

What future in the land of dreamless sleep (Ogham Stone, Limerick City, April 2023)
Flash fiction submission was published in University of Limerick's yearly creative writing journal.

Come Closer (CSIS, University of Limerick, Ireland. November 2022)
This interactive artwork pits the audio and visual representations of a person at odds with one another using an XBOX 360 Kinect as a sensor. Each representation invites the user to approach them, as they do so, the representation being approached dissipates.

Machine North (Liverpool, U.K.. April 2022)
Wrote and produced an original audio piece consisting of a poem and accompanying melody, this was then pressed for a small white label run on vinyl.

Hippo Soop. (Liverpool, U.K., June 2020 - November 2021)
